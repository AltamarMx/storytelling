We are 150 people organized in 50 teams of 3, united by one mission: building a more sustainable world.

But numbers alone don’t tell our story. Let’s scroll through who we really are.

150
people · 50 teams · 1 mission

Our Voices

What does our group look like? Let’s see the breakdown.

68 women (45.3%) form the largest group.

62 men (41.3%) are close behind.

And 20 non-binary people (13.3%) enrich our perspectives. A group is only as strong as its diversity of thought.

What Do We Do?

We come from 12 different career fields. Sustainability needs every discipline.

Software Engineers (28) and Data Scientists (24) lead the pack — we’re a tech-savvy crowd.

Environmental Scientists (18), Designers (16), and Marketers (14) keep us grounded in the real world.

From Public Policy to Agriculture to Journalism — every voice shapes the narrative of change.

Python Knowledge

In the age of data, how well do we speak Python?

35 Beginners (23%) — print("hello world") never felt so good.

55 Intermediates (37%) can wrangle a pandas DataFrame without crying (most of the time). The largest group!

40 Advanced (27%) write decorators in their sleep. 20 Experts (13%) dream in list comprehensions. Together, 77% of us are Intermediate or above.

Favorite Sustainable Development Goals

We asked: “Which SDG is closest to your heart?”

The overwhelming favorite: SDG 13 — Climate Action with 32 votes. The climate crisis connects everything.

Quality Education (22) and Clean Water (18) round out the top 3. Knowledge and water: the two things no one can live without.

Our passions span the entire 2030 Agenda — every goal matters.

What Fuels Us?

Time for the important stuff: what do we drink every week?

2,100 liters of water — enough to fill 10 bathtubs every single week!

525 liters of coffee could fill a small hot tub. That’s 3.5 liters per person — roughly 5 cups a day.

300L of tea, 150L of soda, 120L of juice, and 45L of energy drinks complete the menu.

Fun fact: If we lined up all our weekly water bottles (500ml each), they’d stretch for 2.1 km — the length of 20 football fields!

How Do We Move?

Every week our group covers 15,000 km combined — more than a third of the way around the Earth!

Cars still lead at 6,750 km/week (45%). The biggest slice of our carbon footprint.

Buses & transit at 4,500 km (30%) — public transport for the win!

The green heroes: 2,250 km walked and 1,500 km biked every week — 25 km of zero-emission travel per person.

Challenge: If every car trip under 5 km switched to biking, we’d save roughly 820 kg of CO₂ per week as a group!

Three Words About the SDGs

We asked: “Name three words that come to mind when you think about the SDGs.”

The biggest words reflect our collective consciousness: Future, Hope, and Action dominated.

Together, Planet, and Change tell us something powerful — we see the SDGs as a shared journey toward transformation.

Innovation and Community sit side by side — we believe solutions come from working collectively with creative minds.

If You Were a Renewable Energy…

Time for the real questions. “If you could be a renewable energy source, which one would you be?”

Solar (52 votes) — the crowd favorite! Bright, warm, and available everywhere. Plus, free vitamin D.

Wind (38) — free-spirited and impossible to contain. These are the people who leave meetings early “because they felt a breeze.”

Geothermal (18) — calm on the surface, full of fire underneath. Biomass (14) — literally fueled by leftovers.

Spirit Animal in Meetings

“What’s your spirit animal in team meetings?”

Sloth (35) takes the crown. Slow, steady, and occasionally asleep with eyes open.

Owl (32) — the wise observers. Golden Retriever (28) — just SO HAPPY to be here.

Cat (25) — shows up, judges everyone, leaves. Parrot (18) — repeats everything the manager says. Penguin (12) — looks formal, slides around on their belly.

The Great Plant Census

The question that haunts us all: “How many houseplants have you accidentally… composted?”

Only 22 people (15%) claim zero casualties. We salute you, Plant Whisperers.

45 people lost 1-3 plants — the biggest group. Everyone gets a learning curve.

38 admitted to 4-7 casualties, 25 brave souls confessed to 8+, and 20 simply said “Lost count.” Combined estimated casualties: 487 plants. Moment of silence.

That’s us. 150 people. 50 teams. Diverse careers, varied skills, and a shared commitment to the SDGs.

From the coffee we drink to the kilometers we walk, from our favorite goals to our fallen houseplants — every data point tells a piece of our story.

Now let’s get to work — the future won’t build itself.

(…but at least we have 525 liters of coffee to help.)
